# Plans & Trial

Parametric Memory offers four paid tiers and a 14-day free trial on all of them. There is no free tier — the trial is a fully functional instance at your chosen plan level, with no credit card charge until day 15.

## The tiers

| | **Starter** | **Solo** | **Professional** | **Team** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $3 / month | $9 / month | $29 / month | $79 / month |
| **Atoms** | 1,000 | 10,000 | 100,000 | 500,000 |
| **Bootstraps / month** | 200 (~6/day) | 1,000 (~33/day) | 10,000 (~333/day) | Unlimited (~667+/day) |
| **Storage** | 100 MB | 500 MB | 2 GB | 10 GB |
| **Substrates** | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| **Hosting** | Shared host | Shared host | Shared host | Dedicated droplet |

### What "bootstraps" means

A bootstrap is one call to `memory_session_bootstrap` — the call your AI makes at the start of each session to load relevant context. Each session typically uses one bootstrap. 200/month covers roughly 6 Claude sessions per day; 1,000/month covers roughly 33.

### What "atoms" means

An atom is one named piece of memory — one key/value pair. 1,000 atoms covers approximately 2 months of daily use. 10,000 atoms covers approximately 18 months of daily professional use for a solo developer. You can check your current usage on the Dashboard at any time.

### Overage pricing

If you exceed your atom limit, atoms are charged at **$0.001 per atom** above your tier limit. Overages are reported to Stripe via metered billing and appear on your next invoice. You can set a [spend cap](/docs/spend-caps) to prevent unexpected overage charges.

### Starter vs Solo — which should I choose?

- **Starter** if you are trying Parametric Memory for the first time, or use Claude occasionally
- **Solo** if you use Claude for personal projects, solo development, or moderate daily AI use

### Solo vs Professional — which should I choose?

- **Solo** if you have a single main project and moderate daily AI use
- **Professional** if you run multiple agents, have intensive daily use, or frequently hit the bootstrap limit on Solo

### When to choose Team

Team gives you a dedicated DigitalOcean droplet rather than a shared container. Choose Team when:
- You need guaranteed compute isolation from other customers — your instance runs on its own server with no shared resources
- You want 500,000+ atoms for large-scale institutional memory
- Multiple people on your team will share the same memory instance
- You need up to 5 independent memory substrates for different projects or teams
- You want the best possible response times for bootstraps and large batch operations

**What "dedicated" means in practice:** On shared tiers, your container runs alongside other customers on the same server. Resources are allocated per container, but you share the underlying host. On Team, you get an entire server to yourself — provisioned from a pre-built image for faster startup, running in your chosen region, with full CPU and memory exclusively for your workloads.

## The 14-day trial

Every new account starts with a 14-day free trial at the tier you select at signup.

**How it works:**
- You choose your tier on the pricing page and enter your card at checkout
- Stripe records your card but does **not charge it** until the trial ends
- You get a fully provisioned instance immediately — same limits as a paying subscriber
- On day 15, Stripe charges your card and your subscription becomes active
- You receive an email reminder before day 15

**If you cancel before day 15:**
- No charge is made
- Your instance is moved to read-only status and deprovisioned after a short window
- You can resubscribe at any time

**What happens at the end of the trial if you do nothing:**
- Stripe charges your card automatically
- Your subscription becomes active — nothing changes on your instance

> **There is no free tier after the trial.** If your payment fails at day 15 or you cancel, your instance enters read-only mode. See [Payment Failures](/docs/payment-failures) for what happens next.

## Changing plans

You can upgrade or downgrade at any time from the Dashboard. See [Upgrade Plan](/docs/subscription/upgrade) and [Downgrade Plan](/docs/subscription/downgrade) for the full details of what happens to your instance during each transition.
