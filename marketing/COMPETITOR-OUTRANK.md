# What to borrow from the competition to out-rank them

Grounded in how Mem0, Zep, Letta, and Cognee actually get their traffic (SERP + directory research, July 2026). The pattern: **they don't rank on their own domains — they rank through high-authority third parties (DEV.to, Medium, MCP directories, GitHub) and a shared benchmark.** Copy the channel, beat them on honesty and price.

## 1. Get listed and *claim* your MCP directory entries — fastest win

The category's discovery runs through MCP registries, and they **auto-crawl the ecosystem**, so you may already be listed anonymously. The lever is *claiming* ownership so you control the description and get the backlink. Targets, in priority:

- **Glama** (glama.ai/mcp/servers) — meta-registry co-maintained by Anthropic/GitHub/Microsoft; ~37k servers, "Official/Claimed" tiers. Highest authority.
- **Smithery** (smithery.ai) — the "Docker Hub of MCP"; publish via `smithery mcp publish`.
- **PulseMCP** (pulsemcp.com) — hand-reviewed daily; quality-filtered discovery.
- **MCP.so** and **mcp.directory** — large community registries (the latter already publishes the comparison posts you want to be in).

The open-source **`mcp-submit`** tool pushes to 10+ directories in one command. Each listing is a high-authority backlink *and* a discovery surface competitors are already using. This is the single fastest, most concrete thing you can do.

## 2. Publish a LongMemEval result — the price of entry to comparisons

Every comparison article cites the same benchmark: **LongMemEval** (Mem0 self-reports 94.4%; Zep 63.8% on GPT-4o). Your own probe-based numbers are honest but bespoke — they don't put you on the shared axis, so you're not in the comparisons. Run LongMemEval, publish the number, and you become directly comparable *and* citable. Pair it with your unique axis (verifiability) and you have a benchmark story no competitor can match: "comparable recall, plus cryptographic proof."

## 3. Rank through high-DA channels, not just your own domain

The pages that own "AI agent memory" and "Mem0 vs Zep" live on **DEV.to, Medium, and mcp.directory** — domains that rank in days, not months, because their authority is already established. Your new-site domain can't out-rank them head-on yet, so *join them*: republish your comparison and MCP guide as canonical-linked posts on DEV.to and Medium. You borrow their authority and funnel back to your site.

## 4. Own the "alternative" queries

"Mem0 alternative" is a real, high-intent query, and competitors farm it (EverMind publishes "Best Mem0 Alternatives"). Publish your own **"Best Mem0 / Zep alternatives for AI agent memory"** — honest, featuring yourself with your genuine wedges (verifiability, MCP-native, price). Ranking for a *competitor's* brand + "alternative" is one of the highest-converting plays in the category.

## 5. Ship an open-source hook for GitHub discovery

Letta and Mem0 get enormous developer traffic because their **GitHub repos rank and get starred**. You're closed-source, but you don't have to open the substrate — open a *verifier*, a *CLI* (`mmpm-cli`), and a published *spec* (`@parametric-memory/spec`). A public verifier is on-brand (it proves your core claim) and gives you a GitHub surface that ranks for "verify AI memory," "MCP memory CLI," and links back to the product.

## 6. Get into the existing listicles

The "5 AI agent memory systems compared" posts *are* the category's front page, and you're in none of them. Reach out to the authors (DEV.to, Medium, MCP.directory, atlan, techsy) with your benchmark + honest positioning and ask to be added. One inclusion in a ranking roundup can outperform months of your own SEO.

## 7. Win on the two axes they can't copy

When you show up in any of the above, lead with the two things every competitor lacks:

- **Proof.** None of Mem0, Zep, Letta, or Cognee offer cryptographic verifiability. It's your only truly uncontested claim — make it the headline.
- **Price + no ops.** Graph memory is a $125–$249/mo tier for them; you include it from $5 with nothing to host. That's the wedge the comparison articles will highlight for you.

## Do-this-first order

1. Claim your listings on **Glama, Smithery, PulseMCP, MCP.so** (run `mcp-submit`). — days
2. Publish the **MCP "give Claude memory" guide** and **parametric-vs-non-parametric** post (drafted), then mirror them on **DEV.to + Medium**. — this week
3. Run and publish **LongMemEval**. — 1–2 weeks
4. Publish the **"Best Mem0/Zep alternatives"** comparison, backed by `/benchmark` + LongMemEval. — 2 weeks
5. Open-source a **verifier/CLI** for GitHub discovery. — month
6. Outreach for **listicle inclusion**. — ongoing
