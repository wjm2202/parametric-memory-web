# Name-Collision Analysis: "Parametric Memory" vs. the ML term

## The collision, in one line

"Parametric memory" is an **established machine-learning term** — it means knowledge stored *in a
model's weights* — and it describes the **opposite** of what your product is (external, retrievable,
i.e. *non-parametric* memory). Your brand name competes for a query whose searchers want a concept
definition, not a product.

## The evidence (your own data)

| Query | Impressions | Clicks | What the searcher wants |
|---|---|---|---|
| parametric memory | 248 | 4 | mostly your brand — but ambiguous |
| what is parametric memory | 14 | **0** | the ML concept definition |
| parametric memory meaning | 6 | **0** | the ML concept definition |

The "what is / meaning" queries convert at **0%** because your pages answer "what is the product,"
while the searcher wants "what is the concept." You're being shown for the term and losing the click.

## Why it hurts

1. **Intent mismatch** — concept-seekers bounce (the 0-click rows above).
2. **Discovery** — developers with the problem search *"AI agent memory," "MCP memory," "Mem0
   alternative"* — not "parametric memory." Your name isn't on the path to your buyers.
3. **Credibility risk** — the technical audience that knows the term may read the name as a
   misnomer (you're non-parametric), which reads as either a mistake or marketing spin.

## Recommendation: **add, don't rename**

You have real equity in the name (248 impressions), and a rename resets that and your backlinks.
The fix is a set of *additions* that (a) capture the concept traffic, (b) disambiguate you as a
brand entity, and (c) make you findable by problem. In rough priority:

**1. Own the concept with a bridge page (keystone).**
Publish **"Parametric vs. non-parametric memory in LLMs — explained."** It ranks for the exact
"what is parametric memory" demand you're already shown for, gives the honest definition, and then
positions the product: *"Parametric Memory is verifiable non-parametric memory."* This converts the
liability into your highest-intent educational asset. One page, targeting a query you already
appear for at 0% CTR — the easiest win on the board.

**2. Standardise a descriptor lockup in every title and H1.**
Never ship the bare brand. Use **"Parametric Memory — Verifiable AI Agent Memory"** (or "…memory
for Claude, Cursor & MCP") as the title pattern. This puts category language in front of both
humans and answer engines, so you're findable by problem, not just by name. It also immediately
tells a concept-seeker "this is a product," reducing the intent-mismatch bounce.

**3. Elevate "MMPM" as a collision-free brand token.**
"MMPM" has *zero* term collision — nobody else owns it. Use it consistently as your short handle
(you already do internally). Over time people can search "MMPM" and find only you, giving you a
clean branded query the concept can't dilute.

**4. Add brand-entity structured data.**
Emit `Organization` JSON-LD sitewide with `name: "Parametric Memory"`, `alternateName: "MMPM"`, a
product `description`, and `sameAs` (your X/GitHub/LinkedIn). This tells Google and AI answer engines
to model "Parametric Memory" as a **distinct brand entity**, separate from the generic term — the
mechanism by which the two meanings get disentangled in the Knowledge Graph.

**5. Address it head-on in the FAQ (AEO).**
Ship the FAQ entry *"Is 'Parametric Memory' actually parametric or non-parametric memory?"* (drafted
in `FAQ-additions.md`). Honesty here is a trust signal, and it's exactly the kind of Q&A an answer
engine quotes — which means you get to define the relationship between your name and the concept
rather than leaving it to chance.

**6. Turn the tension into positioning.**
There's a story in the collision: *"Non-parametric memory that behaves like parametric — always
there, and provable."* Your Markov prediction layer literally pre-loads context "before you ask,"
which is the always-there feel of parametric memory, delivered by an external, verifiable store.
Owning that line reframes the name from misnomer to thesis.

**7. Anchor internal + external links with category terms.**
When you link to the homepage or get listed elsewhere, prefer anchor text like "AI agent memory"
or "MCP memory server" over "Parametric Memory," so the pages accrue relevance for the terms that
drive discovery.

## What not to do (yet)

- **Don't rename.** You'd forfeit 248 impressions of equity and your existing backlinks/citations
  for a problem the additions above largely solve.
- **Don't hide the term.** Ranking for "parametric memory" and "non-parametric memory" is an asset
  once the bridge page (#1) exists — you capture concept-seekers and route the relevant ones to the
  product.

## When to revisit the name

If, after the bridge page + descriptor lockup + entity schema are live for ~2 quarters, your
non-branded impressions still won't grow and "MMPM" hasn't taken hold as a searchable token, that's
the signal that the name is a hard ceiling on discovery — and only then is a rename worth the reset.
