## LinkedIn — *audience: enterprise buyers, eng leaders, founders*
*Angle: the shift from reasoning to remembering; verifiability as a control for agents that take real actions. Substance over meme.*

The bottleneck for AI agents is quietly shifting from reasoning to remembering.

Your models can plan, call tools, and write production code. But between two sessions they retain nothing — and the moment an agent takes a consequential action, someone asks the question a chat log can't answer: **what did it actually know when it acted, and can you prove it?**

We've published a technical brief on the memory substrate we built to answer that. The thesis: production agent memory needs four properties at once —

→ a verifiable record of what was known, and when
→ a model of what tends to come next
→ automatic detection of contradictions between stored beliefs
→ a graph of how facts relate

RAG gives you recall. These four give you judgment.

We deliver them behind a single MCP interface — the L2 cache between your model and cold storage: fast, predictive, and cryptographically provable. A log file can be edited. A consistency proof can't.
